## The Contract

The Town of Gilbert recently signed a **five-year contract with Flock Safety** for automated license plate readers (ALPRs), costing taxpayers **$81,000 in the first year** and $72,000 each recurring year.

While marketed as a tool to locate stolen vehicles, these systems capture billions of scans nationwide and store vehicle fingerprints — make, model, color, roof racks, bumper stickers — in a searchable database. **No warrant or probable cause is required to search it.**

---

## The ELSAG Signal Trace Update

While Flock dominates the headlines, defense contractor **Leonardo** (makers of ELSAG plate readers) recently patented a firmware update called **Signal Trace**. This update turns existing camera poles into massive signal intelligence (SIGINT) collectors — **no new hardware required.**

If activated, cameras would sweep and catalog:

- **Bluetooth & Wi-Fi** probe requests from your smartphone
- **MAC addresses and UUIDs** from your earbuds and smartwatches
- **TPMS radio frequencies** from your car's tire pressure sensors
- **RFID tags** from transit passes, work badges, and retail tags
- **Pet microchips**

By combining these signals, the system builds a persistent **electronic fingerprint** of you and your vehicle. Even if you swap your license plate or obscure your face, the system can still identify you. This data is archived indefinitely.
